Baghdad — a living city of history, books, and riverside nights

Baghdad is Iraq’s capital and one of the region’s most culturally rich cities. It blends Abbasid-era heritage, modern neighborhoods, markets, cafés, and a strong local scene centered around the Tigris River.

This page focuses on what travelers actually need: where to go, what to do, how to move around, and practical tips for a smooth visit—without the fake “tour package” fluff.

  • Main Areas

    Al-Karrada, Al-Rasheed Street, Al-Mansour

  • Languages

    Arabic (primary), Kurdish (some), English (limited)

  • Currency Used

    IQD (Iraqi Dinar)

  • Getting Around

    Taxi / local driver (best for multi-stop days)

Top Highlights

If you only have one day, focus on a mix of culture + street life + one landmark. These are common favorites:

  • Al-Mutanabbi Street (books, culture, cafés — best on Fridays)
  • The Iraqi Museum (Mesopotamian heritage)
  • Al-Shaheed Monument (iconic architecture and views)
  • Riverside cafés and evening walks near the Tigris
  • Traditional markets for local food and crafts

Questions About Baghdad

Quick answers to the questions travelers ask the most.

What’s the best day to visit Al-Mutanabbi Street?

Friday is usually the liveliest: book stalls, culture events, and crowds. If you prefer quiet, go on a weekday morning.

Can I get around without Arabic?

Yes, but you’ll need basics: map pins, screenshots, and simple phrases. A local driver or guide makes the experience smoother.

What should I wear in Baghdad?

Modest casual clothing is the safe default. For religious sites, dress more conservatively. Avoid flashy outfits that attract attention.

Is cash needed or can I use cards?

Cash (IQD) is still the most practical for daily spending. Some places accept cards, but don’t rely on it.
